Read it correctly

What “outlier” means

01
Choose a period

We calculate each active stock’s adjusted return over the same window.

02
Measure the group

The mean and population standard deviation describe the peer distribution.

03
Standardize the gap

Return minus mean, divided by standard deviation, gives the z-score.

z = (company return − group mean) ÷ group standard deviation

A large z-score identifies unusual relative movement. It does not explain why it happened and is not a buy or sell signal.

How the index works

Rules are public, calculations are reproducible, and unavailable data is disclosed rather than guessed.

Methodology JSON ↗
01 / Universe

Material eVTOL exposure

Public passenger, cargo, and enabling-platform businesses qualify only when eVTOL is material—not a side project inside a conglomerate.

02 / Eligibility

Size & liquidity screen

Minimum $50M float-adjusted market cap, $250K median daily value, $1 price, and 20 trading days. Failing names stay in the database but not the basket.

03 / Weighting

Free-float market cap

Eligible companies are weighted by float-adjusted market value, then iteratively capped at 15% so one company cannot define the category.

04 / Reviews

Quarterly, point in time

Reviews use data available on the first trading day of each quarter. New listings wait for trading history; delisted companies remain in historical backtests.

05 / Prices

Adjusted and normalized

Daily adjusted closes account for splits and distributions. Foreign primary listings convert to USD; chart series rebase to 100 for fair comparison.

06 / Outliers

Standard deviations

Cross-sectional z-scores reveal unusual leaders and laggards. Rolling annualized volatility shows each stock’s own risk, separately from peer dispersion.
